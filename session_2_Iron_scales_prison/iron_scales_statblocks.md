---

### Prison Lockdown Sequence

**If Alarm Is Triggered:**

**Round 1:**
- Alarm bells ring throughout prison
- Guards mobilize (closest patrol arrives in 2 rounds)
- Warden alerted, begins donning armor

**Round 2:**
- First guard patrol arrives (1d4+2 guards)
- All doors begin locking (DC 20 to force open)
- Mage-Captain begins scrying for intruders

**Round 3:**
- Second patrol arrives (1d4+2 guards + 1 Veteran)
- Warden fully armed, moving toward breach point
- Guard dogs released (2d4 dogs)

**Round 5:**
- Warden arrives with 4 Veteran Guards
- Mage-Captain arrives (flying, casting)
- All exterior gates sealed
- Bridge raised (if applicable)

**After Round 5:**
- Reinforcements keep coming (1d6 guards every 2 rounds)
- Escape becomes very difficult
- Negotiation or fighting through becomes necessary

**Guards Present:** 2 standard guards, 1 veteran (during day)

**If Combat Starts:**
- Guards blow whistles (alarm)
- 1d4+2 additional guards arrive in 3 rounds

---

### The Docks (Supply Entrance)

**Guards Present:** 1 standard guard (bored, less attentive)

**Challenge:** Sneak aboard supply boat or fake delivery (Stealth DC 13 or Deception DC 14)

**If Alarm:**
- Guard blows whistle
- 1d4 guards arrive in 2 rounds
- Boat can be stolen for escape

---

### Cell Block D Entrance

**Guards Present:** 2 veteran guards (always alert)

**Challenge:** Must pass them to reach Ravens' cell

**Options:**
- Talk past them (Deception DC 18 - they're suspicious)

## CHASE SEQUENCE ENCOUNTERS

### Running Through Prison

**Skill Challenge:** 6 successes before 3 failures

**Skills & DCs:**
- **Athletics DC 14:** Climb over obstacles, force through doors
- **Acrobatics DC 13:** Dodge, slide, parkour over furniture
- **Stealth DC 15:** Hide in side rooms, lose pursuers
- **Deception DC 14:** Blend with prisoners/guards, false directions
- **Intimidation DC 13:** Scare guards into backing off, threaten prisoners
- **Sleight of Hand DC 15:** Throw obstacles behind, tangle pursuers

**Pursuers:**
- Warden Grimstone (persistent, never gives up)
- 2 Veteran Guards
- 1d4 Standard Guards
- 2 Guard Dogs (tracking by scent)
- Mage-Captain Voss (flying, casting)

**Consequences:**
- **Success:** Reach escape point with 1 round head start
- **Failure:** Caught and surrounded, must fight or surrender

## SCALING DIFFICULTY

### If Party Is Struggling:

**Make It Easier:**
- Reduce guard patrols (every 30 minutes instead of 20)
- Ravens has hidden lockpicks in her cell
- Friendly prisoner provides distraction
- Secret tunnel is easier to clear (DC 12 Str)
- Warden arrives later (Round 7 instead of 5)

### If Party Is Dominating:

**Make It Harder:**
- Increase guard patrols (every 15 minutes)
- Anti-magic field is larger (40 feet)
- Additional Veteran Guards (2 more)
- Mage-Captain arrives earlier (Round 3)
- Warden has +1 plate armor and more HP (130)

## NOTES FOR DM

### Moral Complexity Reminders

**Key Points to Convey:**
- Wicket seems genuinely grief-stricken (he's a good actor)
- Ravens is professional, not monstrous (just doing a job)
- The truth shouldn't be obvious until the reveal
- No choice is "wrong" - all have consequences

### Pacing Tips

**Act 1 (Planning):** 30 minutes
- Don't rush the meeting
- Let players plan their approach
- Answer questions about prison security

**Act 2 (Breakout):** 60 minutes
- Keep tension high
- Patrols create urgency
- Don't bog down with too many fights

**Act 3 (Reveal):** 30 minutes
- Give players time to process
- Let them debate in-character
- Don't force quick decision

### Roleplaying the Reveal

**Wicket's Breakdown:**
- Starts defensive
- Becomes angry when accused
- Finally breaks down in self-pity (not remorse)
- Offers more money to fix situation

**Ravens' Honesty:**
- Matter-of-fact about the killing
- No apologies, just facts
- Respects party's professionalism
- Will work with them if it helps her escape
